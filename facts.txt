The Leonard P. Zakim Bunker Hill Memorial Bridge carries ten lanes of I‑93 and U.S. Route 1 across the Charles River.  
Source: https://www.mass.gov/info-details/the-big-dig-tunnels-and-bridges

The bridge is 1,432 feet long, with a 745‑foot main span and a deck width of 183 feet, making it the widest cable‑stayed bridge at the time of completion.  
Source: https://www.mass.gov/info-details/the-big-dig-tunnels-and-bridges

Fourteen elephants crossed the bridge on October 14, 2002, to demonstrate its structural strength prior to opening.  
Source: https://www.sec.state.ma.us/mus/pdfs/tourbrochure.pdf

The bridge is named for civil rights leader Leonard P. Zakim and commemorates the Battle of Bunker Hill.  
Source: https://www.mass.gov/info-details/the-big-dig-tunnels-and-bridges
